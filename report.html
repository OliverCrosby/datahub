Birth of a Data Strategy

A report on Website Analytics and User Research

Introduction

As websites go, the data strategy was a small project. Although the design and content of the website were ambitious, the target audience was small. So this report is a bit of an experiment. The goal is to see how much knowledge we can possibly learn from a small web project, focusing qualitatively on the user experience, and to provide some recommendations for future digital projects.

Traffic Overview

We started collecting data on September 2nd. Since then we've seen 582 'new' users, representing roughly 22% of the total staff at PHAC. We don't know for sure how many of those users were 'truly' unique. There could be duplication from users who used different devices, but also a lack of data from browsers that block tracking scripts. This is a rough count of a small sample size, so put away your graphing calculators: we aren't going to be doing any regression analyses here.

Instead we're going to look at the patterns and spikes in traffic.

In the chart above you can see that traffic spiked on several occassions. There are a few observations we can draw from this traffic data:

  1. Traffic did not grow steadily. This is to be expected when most visitors are new. In our case only 26% of visitors returned. This isn’t particularly surprising: for most people, once they’ve seen the data strategy they have no reason to go back.
  2. Traffic spiked on days when we ran announcements in the Broadcast News thingy. This *could* tell us something about the effectiveness of Broadcast News, but we don’t have anything to compare it to.
  3. If you look closely, you can seee that almost nobody looked at the data strategy on the weekend. nobody wants to read about data strategies on the weekend.
