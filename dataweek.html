About the Data Hub

The Public Health Agency of Canada’s Data, Partnerships and Innovation Hub (aka the “Data Hub”) was created in the Fall of 2018 to support PHAC in how we experiment, adopt, and adapt to new data and partnership models to leverage a rapidly expanding ‎information environment both within and outside government. The Data Hub was also asked by Executive Committee to rise to the challenge of the Government of Canada’s Report to the Clerk of the Privy Council: "A Data Strategy Roadmap for the Federal Public Service,” and create a Data Strategy for PHAC. Bringing together subject matter expertise in data already at the Agency, along with many new faces from outside of PHAC, the Data Hub is advancing work to support three pillars: data foundations, data accessibility and data innovation through the implementation of several projects, including those outlined in Phase 1 of the Data Strategy Implementation Plan.

The Data Hub serves as an enabling function within the Agency as it moves into the fourth industrial revolution. The Data Hub will support PHAC in enabling the formation of a governance model for data, more strategic internal and external partnerships, and better access to data. In other words, rather than replacing existing centres of expertise within the Agency, the Data Hub will serve as a conduit to support program areas in accelerating the adoption of modern science-based analytics within their respective areas.

For more information, or for feedback and comments, please reach out to us at PHAC.DataHub-CentreDonnees.ASPC@canada.ca
